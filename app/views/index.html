You can apply for the Healthy Start scheme if either:

  • you are at least 10 weeks pregnant
  • you have at least 1 child under 4 years old

You must also be receiving at least one of the following benefits:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Child Tax Credit (but only if your family’s annual income is £16,190 or less)
  • Universal Credit (but only if your family earns £408 or less from employment)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Working Tax Credit (but only if your family is receiving the 4 week ‘run-on’ payment you receive for a further 4 weeks immediately after you stop qualifying for Working Tax Credit)

You will also be eligible for the Healthy Start scheme if you are pregnant and under 18, even if you do not receive any benefits.

If you are under 16 your parent, guardian or carer must apply for you.

This scheme is only available if you live in England, Wales or Northern Ireland. If you live in Scotland, visit the Scottish Government website for information.

Applying takes around 5 minutes. We will tell you if you are eligible for Healthy Start vouchers straight away.

Before you start

You can also register by calling the Healthy Start helpline on 0345 607 6823. You can find call charge information on GOV.UK.

To apply online or by telephone you will need your:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • National Insurance number
  • baby’s due date (if you are pregnant)
  • postal address

If you live with a partner, you will also need your partner’s:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • National Insurance number

Your details must match the information you gave us when you applied for any other benefits.

If you do not have a National Insurance number, please visit this GOV.UK help page.

The online service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

You can’t register for this service if you’re in the UK illegally.
